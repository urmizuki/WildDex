## Problem Statement and Case Studies
### CASE STUDY 1: DIGITALIZATION OF FOREST INVENTORY SMART-DBH
*(From image_7.png and image_8.png)*
 * **PROBLEM STATEMENT**
   Traditional forest inventory relies heavily on manual labor where foresters use diameter tapes to measure the Diameter at Breast Height (DBH) of individual trees. This method is time-consuming, physically demanding and highly susceptible to human error during data recording in dense tropical terrains.
 * **OBJECTIVE**
   To develop a mobile-based solution capable of automating tree diameter measurements using Computer Vision or Augmented Reality (AR) technology.
 * **TECHNICAL SCOPE**
   * Utilizing smartphone camera sensors to detect and calculate object dimensions.
   * Integrating forestry mensuration formulas to estimate timber volume based on captured data.
   * Implementation of a cloud-based database for real-time digital record-keeping.
### CASE STUDY 2: DIGITAL TIMBER TRACEABILITY CHAIN OF CUSTODY (LOG TRACE)
*(From image_5.png and image_8.png)*
 * **PROBLEM STATEMENT**
   The global timber industry faces significant challenges in verifying the legal origin of wood products. Paper-based documentation systems are easily manipulated, making it difficult to distinguish between timber from certified concessions and illegally harvested logs, thus threatening forest certification standards.
 * **OBJECTIVE**
   To create a digital tracking system that assigns a unique digital identity to each harvested log, ensuring transparency from the felling site to the processing mill.
 * **TECHNICAL SCOPE**
   * Development of a centralized database for log registration and movement history.
   * Implementation of QR Code or NFC/RFID tagging as a physical-to-digital bridge.
   * A monitoring dashboard for forestry authorities to track the "Chain of Custody" in real-time.
### CASE STUDY 3: SMART GEOFENCING FOR FOREST RESERVE SECURITY AND SAFETY
*(From image_2.png)*
 * **PROBLEM STATEMENT**
   Education forest reserves (such as SISFEC) are often subject to unauthorized encroachment and safety risks involving lost hikers. Monitoring vast forested areas manually is inefficient, and delays in locating individuals during Search and Rescue (SAR) operations can be fatal.
 * **OBJECTIVE**
   To develop a digital permit and safety system equipped with Geofencing capabilities to monitor human presence within forest boundaries virtually.
 * **TECHNICAL SCOPE**
   * Integration of mobile GPS/GNSS tracking to monitor user coordinates.
   * Automated "Push Notifications" or haptic alerts if a user enters restricted or high-risk zones.
   * An emergency SOS trigger that transmits location data directly to the forest ranger's command center.
### CASE STUDY 4: AI-DRIVEN SPECIES IDENTIFICATION (THE BIO-IDENTIFIER)
*(From image_1.png and image_2.png)*
 * **PROBLEM STATEMENT**
   Accurate tree species identification in high-biodiversity tropical forests requires specialized expertise in dendrology and wood anatomy. The shortage of experts on-site often slows down biodiversity auditing, forest research, and the enforcement of logging regulations.
 * **OBJECTIVE**
   To build an Artificial Intelligence (AI) image classification model capable of identifying tree species based on bark textures or leaf morphology.
 * **TECHNICAL SCOPE** *(Also visible in image_3.png and image_4.png)*
   * Training a Machine Learning (ML) model using localized datasets of Malaysian timber species.
   * Development of a "Point-and-Identify" mobile interface for field foresters and researchers.
   * Creation of a digital library containing scientific names, conservation status, and ecological data for identified species.
